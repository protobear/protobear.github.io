It's the most wonderful time of the year.

2024-12-03

Merry Christmas and a happy “Please, for the love of all that is good, let my family members read a single book this year so they might form a nuanced opinion on any subject this holiday season, I’m begging”

If there’s one thing I genuinely love about this time of year, it’s the festive events in MMORPGs. When I first started playing World of Warcraft back in September 2007, I had no idea in-game celebrations were a thing. I somehow missed the Halloween event, but stumbling upon Greatfather Winter in Ironforge was a revelation. My eyes were opened to the possibilities these games could bring, namely, An escape from my family.

Christmas to me is a vibe, not a relgious thing. As secular as you can get. I don’t even associate it with relgion most times.

So because ’t is the season let’s take a look at some of my favorite christmas celebrations in MMORPGs. Starting with my absolute favorite, Guild Wars 2.

Guild Wars 2

The soundtrack for Wintersday so so incredibly hard.

The Winter wonderland jumping puzzle is my favorite jumping puzzle. Bar none.

its challenging without being annoying like the clocktower.

its long enough that you feel a bit of relief when you’re done, challenging enough that I don’t do it mindlessly either. I need a couple of tries before its in my fingers. But once its in there, i can run that sucker blind. And I will do it over and over and over again until I’m sick of it. Not for the loot, just because I love it.

Meanwhile I don’t think I’ve ever done Tixx’s infinirarium. Well, I remember doing it once. But that’s so long ago I don’t remember it either.

I played Guild Wars (1) as well, and tbh i don’t remember wintersday there. But wikipedia is assuring me it has it.

Wait

(Googles some things)

oh my god.

Kamadan fully covered in snow. 10/10 Arena Net you’ve done it again.

World of Warcraft

They let themself fall behind a bit in my opinion.

But the random christmas trees everywhere like here in Honor Hold just, idk, its charming in some way. For a brief moment of time the holiday spirit is even on a desolate barren planet. It’s no Guild Wars 1 putting Kamadan in snow moment, but it has its charms.

Some are better done than others, and it made me wish that WoW had a season system.

But 12 year old Marnick, fucking ate this shit up.

Destiny 2

Bungie has some amazing artists, because their branding department is strong.

How the fuck do you make shooting guns feel like Christmas? Also the snowball event in years past was always fun.

Final Fantasy XIV

I don’t really care about the in-game event itself. But I adore all the artwork around it.

It feels like a traditional Christmas.

Honestly their event theme slaps as well

They manage to make it really sound like I imagine to be a traditional christmas
