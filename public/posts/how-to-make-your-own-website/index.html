So, You Want to Build a Website?

There are countless reasons to build your own website: maybe you want to start a blog, create a fansite for something you love, set up a page for your band, or host something completely random—like a live feed of your Christmas tree or reviews of Hercule Poirot episodes. The beauty of the internet lies in its limitless possibilities.

At its core, creating a website boils down to three key elements:

  1. An internet connection
  2. A place to host your site
  3. A domain name so others can easily find it

Before diving in, it’s important to define what kind of site you want to build. This decision will guide your choices later. There are generally two paths:

  • A CMS (Content Management System): Perfect for easily adding posts and pages with minimal hassle. For when you want to create something like a blog using Wordpress, Ghost or something more static like Jekyll.
  • Code it yourself: Offers maximum creative freedom but may require you to learn new skills, like coding or design. But for a website that doesn’t really get regular structured updates it could be the right choice. And it allows you to do some cool things.

Both approaches are valid. One offers convenience, the other offers flexibility. For instance, the blog you’re reading now runs on a platform called Ghost, which I find easy to use and well-suited for my needs. On the other hand, my links page was built from scratch. It’s a quirky little site that looks like what I call “OS X(P),” and the process of creating it was just plain fun. You don’t even have to choose, your server can host multiple sites and services simultaneously.

Learning the skills to build a website, whether from scratch or using a platform, is empowering. It gives you control over your content and eliminates reliance on social media algorithms. Plus, owning your platform means you decide how it looks, functions, and grows.

So, with a bit of patience and curiosity, you too can create your own corner of the internet. Why not get started today?

Getting a Server

Your website needs a place to live. Which means we need a computer that serves you content, a webserver if you will.Ni

Ultimately a webserver is nothing special, just a computer that runs 24/7. It doesn’t need a monitor, keyboard or mouse attached. All it needs is power and ethernet.

If you have a computer that works, is gathering dust, has been made after 2010 and has at least 8GB of RAM you don’t need to make an investment. But I’m going to recommend you do anyway unless you have solar energy or if you’re still not certain because running a computer 24/7 costs money.

And the more powerhungry it is, the most it’ll cost and in the year of our lord Linus Torvalds 2024 we have some really power efficient machines that run great.

I’m going to recommend you get a Raspberry pi 5, 8GB. It’s small, its efficient, it looks intimidating I know but just don’t be a lil bitch about it. You want a website?
